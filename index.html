CRISTIANO RONALDO

Sporting CP Manchester United Real Madrid Juventus

Born on 5 February 1985, Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer who plays as a forward for Premier League club Manchester United and captains the Portugal national team.

Often considered the best player in the world and widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, Ronaldo has won five Ballon d'Or awards and four European Golden Shoes, the most by a European player. He has won 32 trophies in his career, including seven league titles, five UEFA Champions Leagues, and the UEFA European Championship

Sporting CP

The club where it all began.

Started at the age of 16, Cristiano became the first player to play for the club's under-16, under-17 and under-18 teams, the B team and the first team, all within a single season.

United had planned to sign Ronaldo and loan him back to Sporting for a year.Having been impressed by him, the United players urged Ferguson to sign him. After the game, Ferguson agreed to pay Sporting £12.24 million for what he considered to be "one of the most exciting young players" he had ever seen.

A decade after his departure from the club, in April 2013, Sporting honoured Ronaldo by selecting him to become their 100,000th member.

Manchester United FC

Sir Alex Ferguson, also known as the person who found Cristiano, brought him to Manchester United. It was then, that everything started to change for him. Ronaldo was the first Portuguese player to sign for the club.

His transfer fee made him, at the time, the most expensive teenager in English football history. He received the squad number 7 shirt, that became an extra source of motivation for him.

Real Madrid FC

Ahead of the 2009-10 season, Ronaldo joined Real Madrid for a world record transfer fee at the time, of £80 million.

At least 80,000 fans attended his presentation at the Santiago Bernabéu, surpassing the 25-year record of 75,000 fans who had welcomed Diego Maradona at Napoli.
Since club captain Raúl already wore the number 7 (the number Ronaldo wore at United), Ronaldo received the number 9 shirt, which was presented to him by former Madrid player Alfredo Di Stéfano.

During a historic series of four Clásicos against rivals Barcelona in April 2011, Ronaldo scored twice to equal his personal record of 42 goals in all competitions. Though he failed to score in either leg of the Champions League semi-finals as Madrid were eliminated, he equalised from the penalty spot in the return league game and scored the match-winning goal in the 103rd minute of the 2011 Copa del Rey Final, winning his first trophy in Spain.

During the following season, Ronaldo achieved a new personal best of 60 goals in all competitions. He finished as runner-up to Messi for the 2011 FIFA Ballon d'Or, after scoring hat-tricks against Real Zaragoza, Rayo Vallecano, Málaga, Osasuna and Sevilla, the last of which put Madrid on top of the league by the season's midway point

Ronaldo began the 2012-13 season by lifting the 2012 Supercopa de España, his third trophy in Spain. With a goal in each leg, he helped Madrid win the Spanish Super Cup on away goals following a 4-4 aggregate draw against Barcelona.
He became the first player to score in six successive Clásicos when he hit a brace in a 2-2 draw at Camp Nou. His performances again saw Ronaldo voted second in the running for the 2012 FIFA Ballon d'Or, behind four-time winner Messi.

In the season 2013 till 2015, he cnsecutively won Ballon d'Or and La Décima.

From 2015 to 2017, he became all-time Madrid top scorer.

During 2017-2018 he recieved Fifth Ballon d'Or and fifth Champions League title.
